Most online text editors ask you to create an account before you can type a single word. Google Docs needs a Google account. Microsoft Word Online needs a Microsoft account. Notion, Coda, Dropbox Paper — all require sign-up.

Sometimes you just want to write without handing over your email address.

## The Problem With Mandatory Sign-Up

Creating an account means:

- **Sharing personal data** — your name, email, and sometimes phone number
- **Another password to manage** — or another OAuth connection to track
- **Your documents on someone else's server** — stored in the cloud, subject to their privacy policy
- **Marketing emails** — newsletters, upgrade prompts, and feature announcements
- **Account lock-in** — if the service shuts down or changes pricing, your documents are at risk

For a quick document, a meeting note, or a letter draft, this overhead is unnecessary.
