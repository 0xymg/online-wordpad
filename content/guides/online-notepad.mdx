An online notepad is a text editing tool that runs inside your web browser. You open a page and start writing — no software to install, no account to create, and no files to manage until you are ready to save.

## Why Use an Online Notepad?

Desktop Notepad is limited to plain text. Cloud-based editors like Google Docs require a Google account. An online notepad gives you the middle ground:

- **No installation** — open a URL and start typing
- **No account** — your text stays in your browser, not on someone else's server
- **Rich formatting** — bold, italic, headings, lists, tables, and more
- **Works everywhere** — any device with a modern browser (Windows, Mac, Linux, Chromebook, tablet)
- **Free** — no subscription, no trial period, no hidden costs

## How to Use an Online Notepad

1. Go to [wordpad.online/pad](/pad)
2. Start typing — your text is saved automatically
3. Use the toolbar to add formatting, headings, or lists
4. Type `/` to open the command menu for quick access to tables, images, and more
5. Export your document with **File → Export** or press `Ctrl+S`

## Common Uses for an Online Notepad

- **Quick notes** — jot down ideas, meeting notes, or to-do lists without opening a desktop app
- **Writing on public computers** — libraries, schools, or shared workstations where you cannot install software
- **Drafting emails** — write and format long emails before pasting them into your email client
- **Temporary documents** — create a quick document without cluttering your file system
- **Cross-platform writing** — start on your desktop, continue on your tablet

## Is My Data Private?

Yes. Online WordPad stores your document entirely in your browser's local storage. No data is uploaded to any server. There is no backend, no database, and no tracking of your content.
